<br> ###DC motors DC motors are driven by supplying a voltage to the leads, and will reverse if the voltage is reversed. [Machine class 2018 page on DC motors](http://fab.cba.mit.edu/classes/865.18/motion/brushless/index.html) <br> ![pic](./dc_motor.jpg) - Brushed DC motors. Drive with [FET](./../FET/index.html) (single direction) or [h-bridge](./../h_bridge/index.html) (bidirectional) <br> ![pic](gearmotor.jpg) - Geared Brushed DC motors. Slower and more powerful than direct-drive. Drive with [FET](./../FET/index.html) (single direction) or [h-bridge](./../h_bridge/index.html) (bidirectional) <br> ![pic](bldc_motor.jpg) - Brushless DC motors. High performance, high speed, efficient. Require electronic speed control (ESC) modules which receive pulses from a microcontroller (e.g. Arduino) to set the speed. <br> ![pic](vibration.jpg) - A regular brushed motor with an ubalanced rotor makes vibrations.
